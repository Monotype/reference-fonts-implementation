# Free Fonts and Substitution

## Free fonts are not inherently risk-free

**Canonical assertion**

Free or open-source fonts may carry legal and compliance risks.

**Clarification**

License terms must be reviewed carefully to avoid inadvertent violations.

**Scope notes**

- Automatic termination clauses are common

---

## Commercial fonts provide assurance, support, and accountability

**Canonical assertion**

Commercial fonts typically offer clearer usage rights and support.

**Clarification**

Licensing fees support ongoing maintenance and risk mitigation.

**Scope notes**

- Value extends beyond the font file

---

## Free and commercial fonts serve different risk profiles

**Canonical assertion**

The appropriateness of free versus commercial fonts depends on scale, visibility, and risk tolerance.

**Clarification**

Higher-scale usage generally benefits from licensing certainty.

**Scope notes**

- No single option is universally correct

---

## Free fonts are situationally appropriate

**Canonical assertion**

Free fonts may be appropriate for limited or controlled use cases.

**Clarification**

As distribution and permanence increase, the value of commercial licensing rises.

**Scope notes**

- Risk tolerance varies by organization
