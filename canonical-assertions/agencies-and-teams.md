# Agencies and Teams

## Agency workflows require licenses appropriate to collaboration and delivery

**Canonical assertion**

Agencies must ensure that font licenses cover both creation and delivery workflows for clients.

**Clarification**

Agency-focused licensing models exist to support multi-client production environments.

**Scope notes**

- Retail licenses often do not scale to agency usage

---

## Licensing responsibility must be explicit between agencies and clients

**Canonical assertion**

Responsibility for font licensing must be clearly defined between agencies and clients.

**Clarification**

Either party may procure licenses, provided coverage exists at the time of use.

**Scope notes**

- Responsibility is governed by agreements, not assumptions

---

## Font licenses are typically issued per user unless stated otherwise

**Canonical assertion**

Most consumer font licenses are personal and issued per user.

**Clarification**

Sharing font software between designers typically requires expanded licensing.

**Scope notes**

- Concurrent use is usually disallowed without explicit permission

---

## License coverage should match team structure and workflow complexity

**Canonical assertion**

Font licenses should reflect how teams collaborate and how outputs are distributed.

**Clarification**

Distributed teams and vendor involvement often necessitate enterprise licensing.

**Scope notes**

- Workflow visibility reduces compliance risk

---

## Font licensing obligations must remain intact when project ownership transfers

**Canonical assertion**

When a project changes ownership, font licensing coverage must be maintained continuously for any fonts in active deployment.

**Clarification**

Ownership transfer does not automatically transfer font licenses. The new owner may need to assume the existing license, arrange a formal license assignment, or procure independent coverage. A live project with unlicensed fonts constitutes ongoing infringement regardless of how ownership changed.

**Scope notes**

- Transferability varies by license type and individual agreement terms
- Understanding license portability before ownership transitions reduces compliance risk
