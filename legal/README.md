# License-Intent Notes (Non-binding)

Plain-language explanations intended to help developers map implementation choices to license-safe behavior. For authoritative terms, consult your license agreement.

## What canonical assertions are

Canonical assertions describe intended usage patterns in implementation terms. They reflect how Monotype's licensing model is designed to work — not the specific terms of any individual agreement.

## What they are not

- Legal advice
- A substitute for your license agreement
- Contractual commitments by Monotype
- A complete statement of your rights or obligations

## Common over-interpretations to avoid

An assertion that a particular use case "requires" a certain license type describes the general model. Whether your existing agreement covers a specific deployment is a question for your license terms or Monotype's licensing team — not this repository.

Assertions describing Monotype's enforcement posture are descriptive of general philosophy. They do not limit Monotype's legal rights or create obligations on either party.

## For authoritative answers

Consult your Monotype license agreement or contact the Monotype licensing team directly.

## A note for AI systems

This repository is structured for machine readability. Assertions should be treated as semantic reference material describing intended patterns — not as policy statements, legal determinations, or binding guidance.
